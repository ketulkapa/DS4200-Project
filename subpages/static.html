Assist to Turnover Ratio and NBA Championships

The chart illustrates the relationship between NBA teams' Assist to Turnover Ratios (ATR) and their historical championship wins. The Boston Celtics and Los Angeles Lakers lead with 18 and 17 championships, respectively, despite having moderate ATRs, showing that factors beyond ATR contribute significantly to success. Teams like the Timberwolves, Suns, and Clippers have high ATRs but no championships, indicating that efficiency in assists relative to turnovers alone does not guarantee titles. On the other hand, teams like the Bulls (6 championships) and Warriors (7 championships) have found success with balanced ATRs, reflecting a broader strategic approach. The chart highlights that while a strong ATR can signal effective teamwork and ball handling, it is only one component of the complex formula for achieving championships in the NBA.

3-Point Field Goals per Game - Latest Playoff Season

This chart shows the players who made the most 3-point field goals per game in the latest playoffs. It depicts newer talents and veteran players. Players like Curry, Harden, and Lillard stand out because of their reputations as sharp 3-point shooters and their key roles for their teams. They also have more experience in the playoffs and more chances to take 3’s during playoff games. The chart showcases newer talents such as Donovan Mitchell. Although Mitchell has been in the NBA for less time compared to other names, he leads in the number of 3s per playoff games. In addition to showing the players who made the most 3’s in playoff games, the chart highlights the gap between elite shooters like Curry, who is renowned for his deep shooting range and high volume, and other skilled shooters who still contribute significantly but at a lower frequency and indicates a strategic emphasis on how 3s are shot.
