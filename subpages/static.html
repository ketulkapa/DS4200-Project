Assist to Turnover Ratio of the Top 20 Highest Scoring Players

The chart illustrates the relationship between NBA teams' Assist to Turnover Ratios (ATR) and their historical championship wins. The Boston Celtics and Los Angeles Lakers lead with 18 and 17 championships, respectively, despite having moderate ATRs, showing that factors beyond ATR contribute significantly to success. Teams like the Timberwolves, Suns, and Clippers have high ATRs but no championships, indicating that efficiency in assists relative to turnovers alone does not guarantee titles. On the other hand, teams like the Bulls (6 championships) and Warriors (7 championships) have found success with balanced ATRs, reflecting a broader strategic approach. The chart highlights that while a strong ATR can signal effective teamwork and ball handling, it is only one component of the complex formula for achieving championships in the NBA.

3-Point Shooting Evolution in the Playoffs

Some key observation our team found is there is a significant increase for 3 point attempts per game in play-off from 2.0 in 12-13 season to 3.2 for 23-24 season. 3 point made per game is also increase by 68% within the past 12 seasons. The rise of 3-point statistics showing a shift in gameplay strategies over time. Player are adapting 3-point shooting as a primary score method instead of what they did in the past (near basket and pull up jumpers). This has also changed the team dynamic of the transformation of defensive strategies, spacing on the court, and player roles, emphasizing shooters and perimeter play. Due to this transformation, the 3 point percentage is likely to be slightly higher, but the trend may be remaining a stable increase because there are all kind of different defense approach has been change to adapt the transformation of scoring technique.
